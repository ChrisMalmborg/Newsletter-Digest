Fwd: Mozi Minute: Scaling Your Sales Script

Alex Hormozi explains how to create scalable sales scripts by documenting 50+ founder calls using the CLOSER framework, keeping them under 500 words at a 3rd grade reading level, and focusing on script adherence over individual style.

• Sales scripts should be created by having founders/sales leaders take 50+ calls, then using AI tools or manual review to document patterns and create a replicable process that doesn't rely on founder authority.
• The CLOSER framework organizes sales conversations into 6 stages: Clarify (opening), Label (problem identification), Overview (dig into pain), Sell (pitch outcome not product), Explain (handle objections), and Reinforce (decision).
• Effective sales scripts should be under 500 words (4 minutes of talking), written at a 3rd grade reading level, and stripped of anything that doesn't affect close rates to maximize adherence and comprehension.
• Script adherence transforms sales from an art to a measurable process - if the script closes at X%, reps following it will achieve X% over time, making training simpler and results predictable.
